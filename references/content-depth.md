# Content Depth

Avoid shallow concept-list slides. Default depth is `level-3-mechanism`.

## Levels

### level-1-overview
Use only when the user asks for a lightweight overview. Slides can define concepts and show relationships.

### level-2-learning
Explain what each concept means and why it matters. Include simple examples.

### level-3-mechanism
Default. Each content slide must include at least two of:
- mechanism
- contrast
- example
- boundary
- failure point
- practical implication

### level-4-practice
For engineering or work use. Add implementation steps, decision criteria, validation rules, and common mistakes.

### level-5-expert
For advanced analysis. Add assumptions, evidence, edge cases, trade-offs, failure modes, and uncertainty.

## Slide content contract

Except cover and closing slides, avoid pages that only contain labels. A content slide should answer:

```text
What is the claim?
Why is it true?
How does it work?
Where does it fail?
What should the learner do with it?
```

## Anti-patterns

- title plus three vague words
- pure taxonomy without explanation
- too many isolated cards
- no mechanism or decision implication
